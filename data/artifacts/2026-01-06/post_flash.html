6. ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The market is currently navigating a complex landscape dominated by upcoming AI chip earnings, particularly NVIDIA's, which will be a crucial test for the sustainability of the AI boom. Simultaneously, persistent inflation data has dampened expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts, pushing the anticipated first cut to September and leading to a repricing of growth stocks in a higher-for-longer interest rate environment. The unexpectedly soft retail sales data for April further complicates the economic picture, raising concerns about consumer spending and overall economic growth. Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East continue to provide a floor for oil prices and support safe-haven assets. Investors are thus facing a dual challenge: assessing the fundamental strength of the AI sector against a backdrop of tightening financial conditions and potential economic deceleration. The repricing logic centers on two key variables: the terminal value of AI-driven growth and the discount rate applied to future earnings. Any indication of slowing AI demand or a more hawkish Fed stance could lead to further multiple compression for high-growth tech names. Conversely, strong earnings and optimistic guidance from AI leaders could provide a much-needed catalyst. The next two weeks will be critical, with earnings reports and incoming economic data shaping market sentiment. Top tickers to watch include NVIDIA (NVDA) for AI sector health, Microsoft (MSFT) for broader tech and AI infrastructure trends, and Apple (AAPL) as a bellwether for consumer spending and discretionary demand.
